Burnt Pizza

PyroPizza: The Fiery Culinary Adventure

Ingredients:

  • 1 store-bought pizza (preferably one that's been sitting in your freezer since the last lunar eclipse)
  • 1 can of tomato sauce (the older, the better)
  • A handful of cheese (whatever you find hiding in the back of your fridge)
  • Toppings of your choice (or whatever you can scrounge up from your pantry)

Instructions:

  1. Begin by preheating your oven to an astronomical 500 degrees Fahrenheit. Remember, we're not here to bake; we're here to create fiery masterpieces.
  2. Take your frozen pizza out of the freezer, unwrap it, and marvel at its rock-hard splendor. No need to defrost; we're going for the full frosty effect.
  3. Next, grab that trusty can of tomato sauce and apply a liberally uneven layer all over the frozen pizza. Forget about precision; artistic flair is what we're aiming for. Spill some sauce on the countertop for that "happy accident" touch.
  4. Now, it's time for the cheese. Sprinkle whatever cheese remnants you find, but make sure it's so irregularly distributed that you can't recognize the original pizza anymore. A touch of mystery is essential in our burnt-to-a-crisp masterpiece.
  5. Raid your pantry and toss on any toppings you can unearth. Canned pineapple, a few ancient olives, or maybe some crushed potato chips? Anything goes in the world of burnt pizza.
  6. Carefully place your "creation" in the blazing oven and forget about it for longer than you'd ever forget your anniversary. We're aiming for charcoal perfection here!
  8. When your smoke alarms finally start singing an alarm symphony, you'll know it's time. Retrieve your burnt pizza from the oven with a sense of accomplishment.
  9. Serve your Burnt-to-a-Crisp Pizza Surprise on a plate, or perhaps a fireproof tray, and garnish with a stylish cloud of smoke for that extra "wow" factor.
