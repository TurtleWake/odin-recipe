Burnt Fish and Chips

Crispy PyroFish 'n' Chips: A Sizzlin' Adventure (for the Bravest Fryer of the Seven Seas)

Ingredients:

  • 2 fish fillets (any kind, preferably the ones you find at the back of your freezer)
  • 4 large potatoes (the older and wrinklier, the better)
  • 1 cup of oil (extra-virgin motor oil for an extra kick)
  • Salt and pepper (just in case you want to add a little flavor)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to a scorching 450 degrees Fahrenheit. Make sure your smoke alarm is set to "Ready to Party" mode.
  2. Begin by taking those tired, old fish fillets out of your freezer and let them thaw on the kitchen counter for about 10 minutes while you prepare the potatoes.
  3. Next, peel the potatoes (or not, who cares?) and cut them into chunky, irregular shapes. Think of it as modern art on a plate. Once done, toss them onto a baking sheet, but don't forget to forget about them in the oven.
  4. Now, it's time to take the fish fillets and season them with indifference. A sprinkle of salt and a dash of pepper should do the trick, but don't put too much effort into it – remember, we're aiming for burnt!
  5. In a generously oiled, scorching hot pan, place your fish fillets. Leave them untouched for an unreasonable amount of time. Go catch up on your favorite TV show or take a power nap. Only remember to flip them when they're blackened and crispy – that's the "burnt" we're looking for.
  6. Meanwhile, your potato chunks in the oven should have transformed into crispy chunks of oblivion. Take them out, and if they're not charred to a crisp yet, you can always toss them back in for an extra dose of "burnt."
  8. Finally, serve your burnt fish and chips on a plate. Garnish with a decorative plume of smoke and a side of extinguisher for that extra bit of flair.
Enjoy your Burnt-to-a-Crisp Fish and Chips with a glass of ice-cold water, because you'll need it! It's so burnt, it's practically unrecognizable, but that's what makes it hilariously delicious. Bon appétit!
